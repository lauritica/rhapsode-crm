## 2026-04-25 — Pipeline Timeline as the primary view

**Decision:** The Pipeline Timeline replaces the Today List as the daily working surface.

**Why:** Today List worked but only showed me one day at a time. I lost the *shape* of where each client was in the funnel. The Timeline gives me both — I can scan the whole pipeline AND filter to "Today" with a single sort/preset.

**Inspiration:** TV-show cast tables. Rows are people, columns are seasons. I'd been trying to describe this for a year.

---

## 2026-04-25 — Two visual systems, on purpose

**Decision:** The CRM is dark/dense/utilitarian. The brand is warm/editorial/magical-realism. These do not bleed into each other.

**Why:** The CRM is for me. I sit inside it for hours. Warm cream backgrounds get exhausting at hour 4. The brand is for clients — it should feel like a kitchen-table conversation, not a SaaS dashboard. Two different jobs.

---

## 2026-04-25 — Bilingual is not a feature

**Decision:** Spanish appears in product copy as italicized asides, not as a language toggle. Real bilingual code-switching, not "click to translate."

**Why:** That's how Laura actually talks. That's how her clients actually read. A toggle treats Spanish like an afterthought.

---

## 2026-04-25 — No teal, no SaaS chrome, ever

**Decision:** Banned from all customer-facing surfaces: teal, cyan, neon gradients, 3D blobs, "AI swirl" graphics, floating browser-mockup heroes, centered-hero-plus-3-word-tagline patterns.

**Why:** None of that is who we are. The brand is *editorial zine + neighborly real-estate guide*, never *B2B startup*.
