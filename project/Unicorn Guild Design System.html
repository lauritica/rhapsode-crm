01

What we are. What we aren't.

sí
We are

An editorial guide you'd read at a kitchen table.

  • Magical realism — things are real, but a little enchanted
  • Rebellious professionalism — rigor with warmth, not corporate
  • Bilingual, multi-generational, multi-racial by default
  • Rooted in Yellow Springs / Dayton and Latin American texture
  • Systems and tenderness — checklists AND cozy kitchens
never
We are not

A B2B SaaS landing page. Ever.

  • No teal, cyan, or neon gradients
  • No floating browser mockups as heroes
  • No 3D blobs, no "AI magic swirl," no abstract floating shapes
  • No "unlock synergies," no VC deck clichés
  • No centered hero + 3-word tagline + giant purple button
02

03

Type. A literary zine. Not a bank.

Fraunces for headlines — a humanist serif with optical sizes, warm in display and readable in small caps. Figtree for body — a friendly sans that reads like conversation. Caveat for marginalia — the handwritten highlighter moments, used sparingly.

04

Buttons. Round, warm, honest.

Pills, not rectangles. Clay is the action color; espresso is the commit color; ghost and link carry everything else. No drop shadows. No gradients on buttons.

05

Cards. Paper, photo, and step-by-step.

Three patterns. Paper card has a highlighter taped to the top. Photo-led card prioritizes imagery (real when we have it, warm painted gradient when we don't). Step card is numbered and quiet — for buyer journeys and checklists.

06

The system, in one breath.

This is what a section of a first-time buyer one-pager looks like with every piece working together — type, palette, a paper card, a step list, a handwritten margin note.

For first-time buyers · Para compradores primerizos

You didn't miss the boat. you're early, mija —
the harbor just moved.

Most of the stories about buying a home were written for someone else — someone with generational down-payment money, a W-2 going back ten years, and parents who'd done it before. Si eso no eres tú, you are exactly who I built this for. We start at your kitchen table, with a notebook and two cups of coffee.

↳ it's free & 90 min
What we cover
  • i.
    Your real number. Not what Zillow says — what you can actually afford every month, after life. Tu número real, sin sustos.
  • ii.
    A paper map of the next 90 days. Every step, what I do, what you do, what it costs. Un mapa, en papel, para los próximos 90 días.
  • iii.
    Every question you're embarrassed to ask. Bring them all — in English or Spanish, written or out loud. En inglés o español — no hay pregunta pequeña.
  • iv.
    Homework, the good kind. Two sheets of paper, a calendar, and a saved text from me if you get overwhelmed.
